1. What these benefits offer

Residents of Quebec

The Province of Quebec is responsible for providing maternity, paternity, parental, and adoption benefits to residents of Quebec through a program called the Quebec Parental Insurance Program.

Employment Insurance (EI) maternity and parental benefits offer financial assistance to :

  • a person who can't work because they are pregnant or have recently given birth
  • parents who are caring for a newborn or newly adopted child

You could receive up to 55% of your earnings, to a maximum of $547 a week. Some employers may offer additional top-ups. Speak to your employer for more information.

Maternity benefits

Maternity benefits are available exclusively to the person giving birth. They cannot be shared between parents.

The person who receives maternity benefits may also be entitled to additional parental benefits.

Parental benefits

When you apply for parental benefits, you must choose between two options, the standard option or the extended option.

This choice determines the number of weeks and the amount of money you will receive. Once you have begun receiving your benefits, you cannot change options.

Each parent who wishes to receive benefits must:

  • submit their own application
  • select the same option

Special circumstances


Multiple births

The number of weeks of maternity or parental benefits you are entitled to receive does not change if you have a multiple birth (twins, triplets, etc.) or if you adopt more than one child at the same time.

Sickness and bedrest during pregnancy

Although pregnancy and childbirth are not considered to be illnesses, complications with respect to either may be. As long as eligibility conditions are met, a person may receive EI sickness benefits before the birth of a child and transition to maternity and then parental benefits afterwards.

Miscarriage, termination or stillbirth

Maternity leave is for health-related needs surrounding childbirth. A person whose pregnancy ends in miscarriage, termination or stillbirth after the 19th full week of pregnancy is still eligible for maternity benefits.

If a pregnancy ends before the 19th week, they may take apply for EI sickness benefits.

Parental leave does not apply after a miscarriage, termination or stillbirth.

Death of a child

If you are consulting this section following the death of a child, please accept our condolences.

We know that this is a very difficult time for you and we will do everything in our power to facilitate the processing of your file.

Payment of parental benefits ends following the death of a child. If the death occurs while parental benefits are being paid, parents cease to be eligible for the benefits as of the week following the death of the child.
